Role: CTO and Technology Coordinator
Location: Querétaro, Mexico
Event: Congreso y Campeonato Internacional Charro 2015

Duration: 22 consecutive days
Participants: Over 121 teams from Canada, the United States, and Mexico

Responsibility:
- Planning & Implementation: Tasked with planning, purchasing, contracting, implementing, and supervising the launch of the stadium with the most up-to-date technology.

Technology Setup:
- Internet and Wi-Fi networks
- Audio systems

- Closed circuit television (CCTV) systems
- Streaming team for live broadcasts
- Server infrastructure
- Website integration into the platform

+ Historic Streaming: Successfully streamed the event globally for the first time in the history of the Federación Mexicana de Charrería (founded in 1921).

Hello, I am Alfredo Olmedo,

a Senior Site Reliability Engineer (SRE) and DevOps Specialist with over 10 years of experience in cloud migrations, infrastructure optimization, and performance enhancement. My passion lies in designing and maintaining robust, scalable, and highly efficient systems that help businesses thrive in today’s digital landscape.

Throughout my career, I’ve had the opportunity to work in various roles, including SRE, CTO, Software Developer, and Sysadmin, which has given me a deep and practical understanding of technology from multiple angles. I’ve collaborated with organizations of all sizes—from startups to multinational companies—across industries in Mexico, Argentina, the United States, and beyond, driving innovative solutions and optimizing operations.

One of my core strengths is bridging the gap between physical and virtual infrastructure. Whether it’s managing bare metal servers, setting up Ethernet RJ45 wiring, or leading cloud migrations across multiple regions, I thrive on solving complex challenges. Over the years, I’ve successfully reduced operational costs by up to 50% for clients like Virc Snacks LLC and led key technology transformations for organizations such as the Foundation for the Preservation of the Mahayana Tradition (FPMT).

I’m also a continuous learner. With more than 150 courses completed on Platzi and a Bachelor’s Degree in Information Technology and Systems Engineering from UNIVA, I stay at the forefront of emerging technologies and industry trends. This allows me to deliver cutting-edge solutions that not only meet today’s challenges but also anticipate future needs.

Beyond the technical side, I believe in the power of collaboration and community. I’ve actively participated in tech events like IEEE - Mexico CCD Smart Cities of the Future SysArmy, contributing as a speaker and or enthusiastic participant. I’m committed to using technology to create positive, long-lasting impacts, whether that’s by improving infrastructure or contributing to a more equitable and connected world.

If you’re interested in working together or learning more about my journey, feel free to reach out or explore my projects. I’m always open to new challenges and opportunities.

To improve is to learn and apply for the common good
